Neural Network Model

For our analysis and models to be useful we need to have certainty that the assumptions of the modeling techniques are adequately met. The first two models we use are based on linear regression. As mentioned above, there are four major assumptions that must be met in linear regression (Bobbit, 2020) . Our implementation of the linear regression models relies on model diagnostics to check these assumptions. The next section will illustrate that these assumptions were not violated in our analysis. Given this, we believe the linear regression models will be reasonable in their application, regardless of their predictive ability.

Neural networks don’t have the same limitations as linear regression. They impose minimal demands on model structure and that may be in part why they are becoming more and more prevalent. However, the number of cases in our dataset is relatively small (94) compared to many applications of neural networks which may use tens of thousands or a nearly unlimited dataset. This may lead to a neural network that doesn’t have enough training data to draw useful conclusions. Indeed, as our analysis will show, the Root Mean Square Error of the neural network was higher than the linear models’ RMSE. This high error could be in part due to the limited data we fed into the neural network. It could also be a reflection of including the wrong independent variables or not enough independent variables in the network.
