Primary Objective

The primary objective of this study is to explore the capabilities of data science techniques to forecast revenue based on financial data of an advertising agency. To do this, we utilize the data of the local advertising agency Core Creative.

Secondary Objectives

Secondary objectives of this study involve exploring the capabilities of data science techniques to predicting other facets of an advertising agency, again using the data of Core Creative. These other facets might provide predictive capabilities that can be utilized to extrapolate revenue forecasting beyond financial information data sets.

Overview of Core Creative

Core Creative is a full-service branding agency located in the fifth ward of Milwaukee. Their service offerings include public relations, digital advertising, traditional media buying, brand definition services, website development, and design services. Core Creative’s clients come from all across the nation. Most of their client base is in the Midwest, with a large percent of that base located in the greater Milwaukee area. However, they do have a substantial number of clients that are located on the East Coast.

Almost all the work performed for Core Creative clients is on a in progress time and material project basis. Throughout the month employee hours are logged to projects, and costs are classified on the general ledger if they relate to a project. Revenue is then generated by billing back employee hours and project costs to the client at a negotiated rate per employee service activity hour and markup per cost. It is very rare to see a fixed fee billing structure negotiated with a client at Core Creative.

Issues Surrounding the Objectives

The advertising industry is very volatile. Historically core creative experiences a 20% turnover in their client base per year. This is consistent with the industry average. This problem is compounded by the substantial costs to court and close a new client. It often requires substantial investment of time and money to sign a new client. But even at that time there is no contractual obligation for the client to stay with Core Creative. A new business investment might be made to close the deal, only to see that client leave after a small amount of project work. Again, this is consistent with industry standards.

Client demands also make it very difficult to do business in the advertising industry. Clients often want something yesterday for a dollar cheaper than it should be. This is to say, that often a client does not look at their agency of record as a partner, but more so as a vendor. However, the development of complex and strategical advertising plans requires such a partnership. Some projects present unique challenges not foreseen in the initial project budget and require additional time and money from Core Creative. This often leads to write offs when the client does not put in the effort needed to maintain a partnership with Core Creative.

This high paced and volatile environment is not conducive to standard or simple analytical procedures. Factors of inputs change constantly and need to be adjusted. There is often a lot of information about what is going on, but no one has the time to sit down and really dig through it. Additionally, there is not a robust process to examine these changing conditions.

This is where data science comes in. The initial development of data science and programming applications can take time, money, and effort. However, the process can be automated to an extent and can allow for a deeper analysis of the financial relationship between Core Creative and the company’s clients. Data science enables decision makers to identify which inputs are most relevant to Core Creative’s revenue and financial health across the client base and can determine the relative importance of each of those variables. This in turn, can drive decisions that optimize Core Creative’s client relationships, revenue, and employee utilization.
